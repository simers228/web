The Ubermensch Complex
Ordinary vs Extraordinary

I often think about the qualities of the Extraordinary conveyed in Dostoevsky's Crime & Punishment. In today's context, it's less about bloodshed/conquering new territories and more about overcoming regulation. Travis Kalanick, Brian Chesky, and Stephen Kaufer have each put hundreds of thousands of people out of traditional jobs with the founding of their respective companies. However, they are considered extraordinary because they have created many more through collectively innovating a new founded economy built on a primitive singularity: trust. How can we find ways to continue innovating while using new technology and the oldest ideas?
